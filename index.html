Content Strategy & Edited Help Docs

This report looks at categorising content according to structured authoring guidelines. The publicly available help pages on the American QuickBooks website were the source for this college assignment. (Note: Copyright remains with Intuit Quickbooks.) I chose DITA as the structure and created content models for each type of topic (using The Rockley Group’s strategy template). I rewrote several help pages using these content models. (Note: To stay consistent with the original pages, American style has been adhered to, including date format and language usage). I also wrote a guide to organising content into the different DITA topics. The DITA topics were created with Oxygen's XML Editor.

Information Needs Analysis

For the Information Strategy module at CIT I drafted a business needs analysis for the content development cycle of an unnamed med-tech company. The lecturer provided the original interview notes from relevant staff members, and from that I identified the key challenges and gave recommendations, taking the corporate goals into consideration. The business case ‘sells’ the recommendations to management by identifying how I would address the goals and issues with my strategy.
